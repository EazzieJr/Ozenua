Descovering the problem

A user-friendly app is meant to be learnable with the smallest effort invested, unfortunately, the GTWorld app is a bit difficult to use, especially when looking for new features to explore. While I was using the app, I experienced some glitches without understanding why and many other people have highlighted similar challenges as well. Meanwhile, I also needed to hear from other mobile app users to understand what pain points others are experiencing. I scheduled meetings with some users to ask about their experiences and watch them use the app. Some of the issues that were repeatedly reported by users are listed below:

  1. Increased volume of transactional errors.
  2. The app goes blank and that gets users confused.
  3. Navigating around the app is tasking.
  4. DSeveral complain about airtime purchase issues.
  5. Lack of proper error feedback
  6. Other issues include Typography, colors and visual hierarchy

Here are some customer reviews on the Google app store

In addition to this, I also set simple tasks for the users to perform to know if they will be able to seamlessly perform those tasks, I studied their reactions to validate whatever challenges they may have experienced while using the app. I discovered these pain points during the course of the interview.

  1. Users had to pause once they are successfully logged in to find menu options despite using the application before.
  2. Users reportedly conmplained about some part of the app going blank whenever you click on text fields.
  3. The payment page doesn't seem to be intuitive

To further validate my research, I went on to the google play store app to check out what users of the app had to say about their experiences.

Conclusion

The focus of this redesign was centered around delivering an all-new experience on the GTBank application.
